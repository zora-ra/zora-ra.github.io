Capstone Project

LSTM-Based Models for Stock Price Forecast

DATS 6501 - Zixuan Huang

Introduction

Problem Statement

In this research, the objective was to utilized LSTM based models for stock trend predictions. For my study, I have used S&P 500 index values for 20 years as data. An RNN model was used as a benchmark. In comparison, four kinds of LSTM models, including Vanilla LSTM, Stacked LSTM, Bidirectional LSTM and CNN-LSTM were built. In this work, the input was the daily close price of the S&P 500 index, the output of the model was compared with the actual stock price data by using root mean squared error.

Dataset

The raw dataset consists of the following variables: date, the open value of the index, the high value of the index, low value of the index, the close value of the index, and volume of the stock traded on a given date. After dropping missing values, 5273 observations were left. Hence, the training dataset comprised of 4218 records spanning over 843 weeks, and the testing dataset consisted of 1054 records spanning over 210 weeks.

Conclusion

Key Findings

1. LSTM models are better than RNN, and the more complex the model, the smaller the prediction error. LSTM-based models have a much higher capability in extracting and learning the features of time series data than RNN model.

2. The CNN-LSTM model performed the best among these models. The CNN-LSTM model combines the advantages of both convolutional neural networks (CNN) and long short-term memory (LSTM).
